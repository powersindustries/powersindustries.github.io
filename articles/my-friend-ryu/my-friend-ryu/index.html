My Friend Ryu

Apr 11, 2023


As of today, Street Fighter 6 is less than two months away from release. The fighting game community (FGC) is chomping at the bit to get their hands on the game. With the release of several gameplay trailers and two closed beta tests, there is lots of chatter and excitement for the release of Street Fighter 6.

Throughout the buzz surrounding the game, there is one piece of information I’m not seeing anyone talk about. Since the FGC isn’t talking about it, I thought I should since this information is near and dear to my heart.

Personally, Ryu is my favorite character in the franchise. He is my “main” and I love everything about him.

For those who are new to Street Fighter, Ryu is the main protagonist of the series. In Street Fighter’s story, Ryu is an orphaned boy who was raised by his adopted father Gouken. Gouken raises Ryu and teaches him martial arts. At the age of 23, Ryu leaves home and travels the world to hone his martial arts skills. Ryu’s travels eventually bring him to Thailand where he fights his first true adversary Sagat. During their battle, Ryu harnesses the Satsui No Hado for the first time and knocks out Sagat with an almost lethal dragon punch.

The Satsui No Hado can roughly be translated to “Surge of Killing Intent” in English. This Surge of Killing Intent is a dark force that gives its users extreme physical power at the cost of losing their humanity. Just like the dark side of the force in Star Wars, the Satsui No Hado haunts a person until they eventually give into their evil desires.

Throughout the franchise, Ryu is constantly fighting against the Satsui No Hado. At various parts of the story, the Satsui No Hado bubbles up to the surface forcing Ryu to choose whether to harness its power or chose another path. All the way up to Street Fighter 5, Ryu’s inner struggle with the Satusi No Hado takes center stage within his storyline. This dark power hopes to consume Ryu entirely pushing out any good within him (As seen in Street Fighter 4’s Evil Ryu character).

With the background out of the way, let’s get back to Street Fighter 6.

One of the many marketing tools Capcom is using to promote Street Fighter 6 is their website. On the website, each character in the game has a short bio summarizing their storyline leading into the events of Street Fighter 6. Ryu’s biography reads:

“Ever training, this martial artist seeks true strength. Well-mannered and sincere, Ryu travels the globe in search of worthy opponents. Having overcome the Satsui no Hado, he now seeks yet greater heights.”

While reading Ryu’s bio for the first time, I got a little emotional. For the past three decades, my friend has been struggling. He’s been traveling the world searching for the answer to his problem and from the looks of it, it sounds like Ryu finally found the answer he was looking for.

I know that he’s not real. He’s a fictional character in a video game. I know all of that, but he’s real to me. It makes me happy to see that my friend Ryu has overcome his multi-decade struggle and has come out the other end a better man. Judging by the victorious sounding rhythms in Ryu’s new theme, it sounds like Ryu has finally found peace and happiness.

When our loved ones succeed, we succeed. It warms my heart to hear my friend Ryu has finally overcome his inner demons while continuing to seek greater heights.

I can’t wait to see my friend Ryu in Street Fighter 6. Also, I can’t wait to see all of you in the streets of Metro City.

Cheers everyone!
